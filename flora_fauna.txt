Langtang National Park (1,710 km2) and buffer zone (420 km2)

WDPA Site code 803 and Site code: 313458

Langtang National Park (over 6450m) is one of the greatest altitudinal ranges within its boundaries. 
The Park is linked with Qomolongma Nature Preserve in the Tibet Autonomous Region of China, and is famous for the 

religious shrines like Gosainkund, 
and 15 species of endemic flowering plants. 

45 mammals, 
Snow leopard (Uncia uncia), Clouded leopard (Pardofelis nebulosa), Musk deer (Moschus chrysogaster), and Red panda (Ailurus fulgens)
345 birds, 
Impeyan pheasant, Ibis bill, White-winged redstart, and Snow partridge
11 herpeto, 
and 30 fish species. 
The number of vascular plants recorded in the Park is 1,043 (67 pteridophytes, 10 gymnosperms, 818 dicots, and 148 monocots).

Current checklists include 45 mammals, 345 birds, four herpeto, and two fish species (Annex 2.16)

3,689 species of flowering plants were expected in Langtang National Park; 
the reported number is much less: 976
Rhodondendron cownianum, R. lowndesii, and Larix nepalensis


Park landscape consists of mountain peaks, glaciers, high altitude lakes, rivers, pastures, and forests
 
Estimated 3,689 species of flora are native to
the Park (BPP 1995)
